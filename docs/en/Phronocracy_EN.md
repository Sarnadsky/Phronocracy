# Phronocracy

**Author:** Nikita Sarnadsky  
**Years of development:** 2025–2026  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 2026

## Abstract

Phronocracy is a proposed model of state governance in which the legitimacy of power arises solely from the empirically verified ability to make accurate forecasts and achieve stated results under conditions of high uncertainty and systemic complexity.

Drawing on Aristotle's concept of phronesis (practical wisdom), the model institutionalizes competition among managerial hypotheses, rigorous reality-based verification, a reputational hierarchy, and absolute personal responsibility - including severe sanctions for catastrophic incompetence.

---

## Etymology

The term "phronocracy" is formed from the ancient Greek phronesis - "practical wisdom", understood as the ability to make informed decisions under conditions of uncertainty, and kratos - "power", "governance". In the Aristotelian tradition, phronesis is contrasted both with purely theoretical knowledge (sophia) and with formal scientific rationality (episteme), emphasizing contextuality, temporal limitation and practical verifiability of decisions.

The name of the concept fixes its fundamental principle: power belongs to managerial models that demonstrate stable prognostic adequacy when tested by reality.

## Definition

Phronocracy is a political model of governance for complex socio-economic systems, based on institutionalized competition of managerial subjects and their evaluation through publicly recorded forecasts with subsequent empirical verification.

Phronocracy is a form of state organization in which the legitimacy of power is produced automatically through demonstration of effectiveness. Power is legitimate as long as it proves its ability to make accurate forecasts and achieve stated results.

## Methodological Note on Quantitative Indicators

All quantitative indicators in this concept (percentage distribution of circuits, responsibility thresholds, time horizons, forecast accuracy thresholds) are illustrative examples based on generalization of historical experience of managerial systems and observed distribution of competencies in modern societies.

Specific values must be determined empirically for each jurisdiction taking into account:
- Education level of the population
- Technological development
- Specifics of tasks being solved
- Cultural context
- Size and complexity of the managed system

The figures provided are not rigid constitutional requirements, but represent a starting point for calibrating the system under conditions of specific implementation.

## Source of Primary Legitimacy

Phronocracy does not create legitimacy ex nihilo. Primary legitimacy is borrowed from the existing order through one of the establishment mechanisms. The goal-setting body inherits legitimacy from the preceding system through constitutional continuity, popular vote, international recognition or institutional continuity.

After establishment, phronocracy produces secondary legitimacy through demonstration of effectiveness. Over time, secondary legitimacy replaces primary legitimacy in public perception. If effectiveness is not confirmed, the system loses secondary legitimacy, which leads to its replacement through constitutional mechanisms or crisis restructuring.

Phronocracy does not solve the philosophical problem of absolute justification of power. It uses the existing legitimation foundation and builds on top of it a system of continuous verification of competence.

## Theoretical Premises

The concept of phronocracy is based on the following observations:

1. **Growth of systemic complexity.** Modern socio-economic systems are characterized by a high degree of nonlinearity, multiple feedback loops and sensitivity to initial conditions, which reduces the effectiveness of traditional forms of planning.

2. **Ineliminable uncertainty.** In many managerial contexts, it is impossible to obtain complete information or unambiguously determine the optimal solution before its implementation.

3. **Structural hostility of existing systems to competence.** Democracies select the popular, autocracies - the loyal, oligarchies - the wealthy. None of these systems selects the effective.

Under these conditions, phronocracy shifts the focus of governance from searching for the right people to testing specific hypotheses about the future state of the system.

## Basic Principles

### Prognostic Formalization

Every managerial decision is accompanied by an explicit forecast of its consequences. The forecast includes a time horizon, a set of measurable indicators, expected value ranges and conditions under which the forecast is considered confirmed or refuted. Forecasts cannot be changed or reinterpreted post factum.

### Verification Through Reality

After the expiration of the stated time horizon, forecasts are compared with actual results. Comparison procedures and metrics used are fixed in advance in an unchangeable format. The discrepancy between the forecast and the result is recorded as a management error.

### Distinguishing Competent Management from Self-Fulfilling Effects

The publicity of forecasts creates a risk of self-fulfilling prophecies: the forecast influences people's behavior, which in itself can fulfill or destroy the forecast regardless of the manager's actions.

Phronocracy accounts for this effect through division of forecasts into two categories:

- **Managerial forecasts:** prediction of results of the subject's own actions. Example: "Implementation of an infrastructure project will lead to growth of gross regional product by a certain percentage over several years". Here the publicity of the forecast does not directly affect the result - what matters are real actions to implement the project.

- **System state forecasts:** prediction of behavior of external factors or agents. Example: "Inflation will be at a certain level next year", "Bank X will go bankrupt in a year". Here publicity can create a self-fulfilling effect.

For managerial forecasts, the standard verification procedure applies: comparing the forecast with the result. For system state forecasts, causal relationships are additionally analyzed: was the forecast confirmed due to adequate understanding of the system or due to agents' reaction to the forecast itself.

Analysis is conducted retrospectively by an independent commission using counterfactual modeling: what would have happened if the forecast had not been published. When a dominant self-fulfilling effect is detected, the forecast is counted with reduced weight in the reputation rating.

Subjects who systematically use self-fulfilling forecasts instead of real management receive a reputational penalty for manipulating the evaluation system.

### Competition of Managerial Subjects

Phronocracy allows simultaneous existence and comparison of alternative managerial models under comparable conditions. Advantage goes to the subject demonstrating higher prognostic accuracy.

#### Who can submit an alternative forecast

Any subject of the executive or strategic circuit can submit an alternative forecast on any issue within their area of competence or a related field. A subject of the supporting circuit can submit an alternative forecast only after passing the admission procedure to the executive circuit.

#### Submission procedure

An alternative forecast is recorded in the same format as the main one: time horizon, measurable indicators, value ranges, verification conditions. Additionally, the applicant describes differences of their approach from the current model and justifies why they expect a different result.

#### Resource allocation

The decision on resource allocation is made by the strategic circuit based on the following criteria: presence of reputational history for the applicant (for new subjects - results of the trial period), validity of the alternative approach (presence of theoretical basis, analogues, expert support), comparability of verification conditions (possibility to create a controlled experiment).

The volume of allocated resources is proportional to the scale of the hypothesis being tested: for local experiments (district, enterprise, short time horizon) - minimal resources with quick verification; for regional or sectoral experiments - larger volume of resources with a longer horizon; for strategic alternatives at national level - access is possible only for subjects with confirmed reputation at lower levels.

#### Comparability of conditions

Alternative models are tested in parallel under maximally identical conditions. For territorial management: regions with similar levels of development, demographics, resource base. For sectoral management: enterprises of the same profile, size, market niche. Differences in starting conditions are recorded and taken into account when interpreting results.

#### Responsibility

The applicant of an alternative forecast assumes full responsibility for results to the same extent as the current manager. When the alternative forecast is confirmed, the applicant receives priority in managing the corresponding area. In case of failure - demotion according to the graduated responsibility system.

If the current and alternative models show comparable results (difference within statistical error), resource efficiency is evaluated: which model achieved the result with lower costs. With equal efficiency, priority remains with the current model by virtue of the stability principle.

### Reputational Hierarchy

Verification results are accumulated in the form of a long-term reputational indicator reflecting the historical accuracy of forecasts by the managerial subject. Reputation determines access to resources, scale of responsibility zones and the right to strategic decisions. Reputation is not inherited, not transferred and not bought.

### Absolute Responsibility

Phronocracy uses a graduated responsibility system depending on the severity and systematic nature of errors.

**Single error of medium severity** (forecast not confirmed, but consequences not critical): demotion by one hierarchical level with partial retention of reputation. A subject from the strategic circuit is transferred to the executive, from the executive - to the supporting. The right to re-attempt transition is retained after an established period of confirming new competencies.

**Systematic errors** (multiple critical discrepancies between forecast and result over an established period, or stable prognostic accuracy below threshold value): return to the supporting circuit regardless of current level. Reputation is reset to zero. The right to re-attempt access to management is possible after an established period provided competencies are confirmed in a new subject area.

**Catastrophic consequences of incompetence:** complete lifelong exclusion from any managerial positions. Additional sanctions are determined by the goal-setting body depending on constitutional limitations of the jurisdiction and include a range from property confiscation and life imprisonment to physical elimination.

#### Definition of catastrophic consequences

The catastrophic threshold is established constitutionally for each jurisdiction individually and may include such criteria as:
- human deaths (absolute number or percentage of population of the managed territory)
- economic damage (absolute value or percentage of gross regional product)
- irreversible destruction of critical infrastructure (life support systems, healthcare, communications)
- environmental damage with long-term consequences (pollution, loss of biodiversity)

Specific threshold values are calibrated depending on the size of the jurisdiction, level of development, typical risks. For a small city, the threshold for human casualties will mean a catastrophe of a different scale than for a large country. The constitution must account for this scale dependence.

#### Distinguishing incompetence from uncertainty

Responsibility occurs only for errors of incompetence, not for errors of uncertainty.

**Error of incompetence:** the subject had access to information allowing avoidance of consequences, but did not use it; the subject ignored warnings from experts or alternative forecasts; the subject acted contrary to established procedures without justification; the subject concealed negative information from higher instances or the verification circuit.

**Error of uncertainty:** the event was fundamentally unpredictable based on available information (natural disaster of new type, competitor's technological breakthrough); the subject indicated the risk in advance in the forecast and described the range of possible outcomes; the subject acted in accordance with best available practices; the event could not have been prevented by alternative decisions based on information available at the time of decision-making.

The distinction is made by an independent commission of representatives from all three circuits with the right to involve external experts. The burden of proof lies with the prosecution: by default, an error is considered a consequence of uncertainty until proven otherwise. The subject has the right to public defense and provision of all documents confirming the reasonableness of the decision made at the time of its adoption.

### Separation of Goals and Means

Phronocracy separates goal-setting from implementation. Goals, priorities and normative constraints are set by a constitutionally established goal-setting body, formed through mechanisms not connected with the phronocratic hierarchy. The phronocratic circuit is responsible exclusively for selecting and testing means of achieving set goals.

Phronocracy does not solve the problem of determining correct goals. This problem is fundamentally unsolvable by technical means and always requires normative choice. The separation of goal-setting and execution is not an attempt to hide the problem, but recognition of its ineliminability.

The goal-setting body can err in choosing metrics. But phronocracy guarantees that with any metrics, the most effective means of achieving them will be chosen. This is more honest than systems where the same subjects determine both goals and means and criteria for evaluating their own effectiveness.

## Structure of Power

Phronocracy is organized into three functional circuits with explicit hierarchy of competencies. The percentage distribution is an approximate estimate based on observation of historical managerial structures: the Chinese mandarin system encompassed approximately an insignificant portion of the population; modern professional classes (scientists, engineers, doctors, managers) constitute a significant but smaller part of the employed population in developed economies; the rest of the population is engaged in algorithmizable labor.

These figures are not constitutionally fixed constants. Actual distribution is determined by the real distribution of confirmed competencies at the time of implementation and may differ significantly. In a highly educated society, the executive circuit may constitute a significantly larger share. In a society with a low level of formal education but a high level of practical skills - the distribution may be different. What matters is the principle: access is determined by confirmed ability, not by predetermined quotas.

### Strategic Circuit (small part of the population)

Subjects who have proven ability for macro-design, systemic analysis and decision-making with a planning horizon of ten years or more. Access to the strategic circuit is possible only through sequential confirmation of results at all lower levels.

**Access requirements:** confirmed prognostic accuracy not lower than the constitutionally established threshold value on a long-term horizon, demonstration of successful management of systems of increasing complexity, absence of catastrophic failures in managerial history, ability for long-term strategic planning (confirmed through specialized tests and expert evaluation).

**Powers:** determination of strategic development vectors within constitutionally set goals, resource distribution among executive circuits, appointment and removal of executive circuit leaders.

**Responsibility:** absolute according to the graduated system. Strategic errors are punished depending on their severity and systematic nature.

### Executive Circuit (significant part of the population)

Subjects possessing high specialized competence: scientists, engineers, doctors, middle-level managers, analysts. Access through confirmation of expertise and managerial results at the operational level.

**Access requirements:** confirmed expertise in the subject area, managerial experience, prognostic accuracy not lower than the constitutionally established threshold value on a medium-term horizon.

**Powers:** implementation of strategies, management of processes and resources within delegated powers, advancement of alternative solutions when disagreeing with the strategic circuit.

**Responsibility:** demotion in case of failure, return to the supporting circuit in case of systematic errors.

### Supporting Circuit (large part of the population)

Subjects performing standardized, algorithmizable work. Access is automatic for all citizens not belonging to higher circuits.

**Access requirements:** none.

**Powers in the managerial hierarchy:** none.

**Guaranteed rights:** physical security, basic material support, access to standardized services, possibility of transition to the executive circuit through confirmation of competencies.

**Responsibility:** none for managerial decisions, as they are not admitted to them.

### Mechanism for Protecting Rights of the Supporting Circuit

The goal-setting body is formed with mandatory significant representation from the supporting circuit. Representatives are selected through lottery or rotational elections with limited term of office. This guarantees that basic rights and material support cannot be curtailed without the consent of the governed themselves.

The supporting circuit does not participate in operational decisions (how to manage), but participates in goal-setting (what is a priority, what basic guarantees are inviolable). This separates strategic competence from normative control.

Additionally: any subject of the supporting circuit can file an appeal to an independent constitutional court in case of violation of guaranteed rights. The court is formed on a parity basis from representatives of all three circuits and has the right to block decisions of the phronocratic hierarchy that contradict constitutional guarantees.

### Enhanced Protection from Domination by Qualified Minority

Although representatives of the supporting circuit constitute a significant share of the goal-setting body, there is a risk of systematic advantage of qualified representatives in all votes affecting basic rights.

To compensate for this imbalance, the **qualified minority rule** is introduced: any decision reducing constitutional guarantees of the supporting circuit (material support, physical security, access to basic services, right of transition to the executive circuit) requires approval of at least half of the representatives from the supporting circuit.

This means that to adopt such decisions, general majority or even qualified majority is not sufficient - it is necessary that half of the representatives of the supporting circuit itself support the change.

Additionally: the distinction between "WHAT" (goal-setting) and "HOW" (operational management) is defined as follows:

**"WHAT"** - decisions affecting constitutional guarantees, development priorities, resource distribution among fundamental directions (defense, healthcare, education, infrastructure), changes in procedures and evaluation metrics. These decisions are made by the goal-setting body.

**"HOW"** - decisions on specific methods of achieving set goals, choice between alternative technologies or approaches, operational redistribution of resources within a direction, appointment of specific executors. These decisions are made by the phronocratic circuit.

Disputed cases (unclear whether a decision relates to "WHAT" or "HOW") are resolved by the constitutional court, which is formed on a parity basis but with enhanced representation of the supporting circuit. In case of a tie, the deciding vote belongs to a randomly selected judge from the supporting circuit.

## Institutional Organization

### Goal-Setting Body

Formed through a constitutionally established mixed mechanism, independent of the phronocratic hierarchy:

**Significant share - representatives of the supporting circuit.** Selected through random lottery from among citizens meeting basic requirements: adulthood, absence of convictions for serious crimes, permanent residence in the jurisdiction. The lottery is conducted publicly using a cryptographically protected random selection algorithm. Selected citizens may refuse participation, in which case a repeat lottery is conducted.

**Smaller share - representatives of the executive circuit.** Selected through voting within subject areas: healthcare, education, industry, agriculture, science, infrastructure and others. Each area delegates a fixed number of representatives proportional to the number of specialists. Voting is conducted among all members of the executive circuit of a given area. The candidate must have above-average reputation and not be a current leader in the strategic circuit (to exclude conflict of interest).

**Smaller share - representatives of the strategic circuit.** Selected by reputation rating: candidates with the highest prognostic accuracy indicators over an extended period. Appointment is automatic based on public rating without possibility of refusal (membership in the goal-setting body is a constitutional obligation of the strategic circuit).

**Small share - external observers.** Delegated by academic and research institutes not part of the phronocratic state structure: foreign universities, international scientific organizations, independent research centers. The specific list of institutions having the right of delegation is enshrined in the constitution and can be changed only by qualified majority of the goal-setting body itself. External observers do not have voting rights on issues of adopting or repealing goals, but have voting rights on issues of changing procedures and metrics.

#### Powers of the body

The body determines constitutional goals, development priorities, normative constraints, basic guarantees for all circuits. The body can change the reputation calculation algorithm and verification procedures by qualified majority. The body does not make operational decisions and cannot interfere in current management.

#### Limitations of powers

The body cannot change procedures of its own formation by simple majority. Changing composition, proportions or selection mechanisms requires conducting a nationwide referendum with high participation and approval thresholds.

The body cannot be dissolved by the phronocratic circuit under any circumstances. In case of emergency (war, natural disaster, epidemic), the body can delegate expanded powers to the strategic circuit, but delegation is limited by time frames and automatically terminates upon expiration of the term or by vote of qualified majority of the body.

#### Term of office

Extended period with gradual rotation of part of the composition to ensure continuity and renewal. Rotation occurs proportionally across all quotas. Repeat membership in the goal-setting body is possible after an established period following the end of the previous period.

### Forecast Recording System

Distributed ledger with cryptographic protection against changes. Each forecast is recorded with a timestamp, author, metrics and verification conditions. Access to the ledger is public and unlimited. The ledger is duplicated on independent servers in different jurisdictions to protect against censorship or manipulation.

### System of Protection Against Metric Manipulation

Any metric, having become a management goal, ceases to be a reliable metric (Goodhart's law). Phronocracy neutralizes this through multi-level protection:

**Multiplicity of measurement methods.** Each key indicator is measured by several independent methods. For example, population health level is measured through: official medical statistics, independent epidemiological studies, insurance data on morbidity. Significant discrepancy between methods triggers an independent investigation.

**Second-order metrics.** For each direct metric, there are indirect indicators that should correlate with the main one. If the declared goal is to increase education level through test scores, second-order metrics are: scientific publications by graduates after several years, percentage of employment in specialty, wage premium for education. Growth of test scores without growth of second-order metrics indicates manipulation.

**Random independent audit.** A significant share of all measurements is duplicated by an independent group, randomly selected shortly before verification. The group uses alternative data collection methods (different sample, different instruments, different time of conduct). Significant discrepancy between official data and independent verification entails investigation and temporary suspension of the subject's access to reporting until causes are clarified.

**Ban on control of the measurement process.** The subject reporting on a metric does not have the right to influence the process of its measurement. Institutions conducting measurement (statistical services, research centers, audit organizations) are subordinate directly to the goal-setting body, not to the managerial circuit. An attempt to influence measurement methodology or personnel of measuring institutions is qualified as falsification and entails corresponding sanctions according to the graduated responsibility system.

**Publicity of raw data.** All primary data on which aggregated indicators are calculated are published in open access without processing. Any citizen or independent researcher can recalculate final indicators and point out discrepancies. Detection of systematic data distortion is materially rewarded.

**Whistleblower protection.** Any person who provides evidence of metric manipulation receives full protection from prosecution, anonymity preservation for the entire period of investigation and material compensation when information is confirmed. An attempt to identify or punish the informant is punished as complicity in falsification.

### Verification Circuit

Independent system for checking correspondence of results with forecasts. Formed through random selection from the executive circuit for a limited term without right of extension. Remuneration for detecting falsifications constitutes a significant share of confiscated resources of the eliminated subject. Missing a falsification entails demotion of the auditor to the supporting circuit.

#### Independence of Verification Circuit from Strategic Circuit

Auditors are selected randomly from the executive circuit, but the selection procedure excludes the possibility of influence from the subjects being audited. The pool of potential auditors is formed automatically from members of the executive circuit meeting criteria: above-average reputation, absence of direct subordination to the subject being audited for an extended period, absence of family or business ties with the subject being audited.

Appointment of a specific auditor for a specific audit occurs shortly before the start of the audit using a cryptographically protected random number generator. The selection algorithm is public and cannot be changed without a decision of the goal-setting body.

The auditor does not know in advance whom exactly they will audit until the moment of appointment. After appointment, any contact between the auditor and the subject being audited, not directly related to the audit procedure, is prohibited under threat of return of both parties to the supporting circuit.

Career advancement of the auditor does not depend on decisions of the strategic circuit. Evaluation of auditors' work is produced collegially by other auditors and the goal-setting body based on accuracy and validity of their conclusions, not on loyalty to the subjects being audited.

#### Multi-Level Verification of Auditors

The verification circuit itself is subject to verification through a system of mutual control and random re-audits.

Each audit is duplicated by a second independent auditor, appointed randomly. Auditors do not know about each other until the moment of providing results. Discrepancy in conclusions by more than the error threshold triggers a third independent audit.

Additionally: a random portion of the total number of audits is selected for re-audit after a certain period following the initial audit. The re-audit is conducted by a group of several auditors selected from another region or agency. If the re-audit discovers falsification missed by the initial auditors, all participants of the initial audit are demoted by one circuit.

Public control: any citizen can request source data from any audit and conduct their own analysis. When discrepancies are detected, the citizen submits an application to an independent appeals commission. If the discrepancy is confirmed, the citizen receives material remuneration, and responsible auditors are demoted.

An attempt at collusion between auditors is qualified as falsification and entails return of all participants to the supporting circuit with prohibition of re-access to management for an established period.

### Reputation Accumulation System

Automated platform aggregating verification results into a unified reputational indicator. The calculation algorithm is constitutionally fixed and can be changed only through the goal-setting body by qualified majority. Reputation is public and accessible for verification by any citizen.

Reputation accounts for: short-term accuracy, long-term accuracy, correspondence of stated to actual with different weights for each component. The formula is open and contains no hidden parameters.

### Procedures for Admission to Management

#### Transition from Supporting Circuit to Executive

Carried out through a system of professional certification and confirmation of results during a trial period. The candidate provides evidence of specialized competence: education, professional experience, publications, patents, recommendations from recognized experts.

After confirmation of competence, the candidate receives a trial period during which they perform real managerial tasks under supervision. The candidate formulates forecasts of their work results in accordance with general phronocracy requirements.

Upon expiration of the trial period, results are compared with forecasts. With prognostic accuracy above the threshold value, the candidate is transferred to the executive circuit with assignment of initial reputation. With accuracy below the threshold - returned to the supporting circuit with the right to re-attempt after an established period.

#### Transition from Executive Circuit to Strategic

Requires confirmation of ability for systemic thinking, long-term planning and management under conditions of high uncertainty. Access is possible only through sequential confirmation of results at all lower levels.

**Access criteria:** confirmed prognostic accuracy not lower than the established threshold on a long-term horizon, demonstration of successful management of systems of increasing complexity (from local level to regional), absence of catastrophic failures in managerial history, ability for long-term strategic planning (confirmed through specialized tests and expert evaluation by colleagues from the strategic circuit).

Transition does not require a fixed period of stay in the executive circuit. The subject can claim transition at any time when meeting all criteria. The decision is made collegially by current members of the strategic circuit based on public evaluation of the candidate's reputational history.

#### Demotion Between Circuits

In case of a single error of medium severity, the subject is demoted by one level: from strategic to executive, from executive to supporting. Reputation is partially retained (decreased, but not completely reset).

Demotion does not mean impossibility of re-advancement. The subject can return to the previous level after reputation restoration through confirmation of results at the current level. Re-transition criteria are analogous to primary ones, but require demonstration of result stability over a longer horizon to compensate for the previous error.

In case of systematic errors, the subject returns to the supporting circuit regardless of initial level. Reputation is completely reset. The right of re-access to management is possible after an established period provided competencies are confirmed in a new subject area.

## Establishment Mechanism

Phronocracy is established under conditions of systemic crisis of the existing order through one of three scenarios:

### Institutional Capture

Gradual introduction of phronocratic principles of elite selection into existing state structures. The process begins with technical agencies (healthcare, education, infrastructure), where results are easily measurable.

Initiators introduce a system of public forecasts and result verification in individual departments or regions as experimental programs. Successful examples create pressure on political structures to expand the principle to adjacent areas.

Critical mass is achieved when controlling a significant share of key positions (ministers, regional heads, leaders of largest state corporations). At this stage, supporters of the phronocratic approach gain the ability to initiate constitutional reform through legitimate procedures (referendum, parliamentary majority, head of state support).

The scenario requires an extended period and is possible only in systems with relatively low levels of corruption and functioning institutions.

### Parallel Construction

Creation of managerial structures based on phronocratic principles in zones where the state delegated management or agreed to an experiment: economic free zones, technology clusters, special administrative districts, territories with autonomous status.

Initiators receive permission to apply an alternative system of manager selection and work evaluation. Key condition: direct comparability of results with traditionally managed territories (identical starting conditions, identical measurement metrics).

If experimental territories demonstrate stable superiority maintained over an extended period, this creates pressure on the central government to expand the experiment. Citizens and investors begin to prefer experimental jurisdictions.

Legitimacy switching occurs either through voluntary adoption of phronocratic principles by the existing state, or through expansion of autonomy of experimental territories to de facto independence.

The scenario requires an extended period and is possible in federal states, countries with special economic zones or states ready for managerial experiments.

### Crisis Replacement

Establishment of phronocracy at the moment of collapse of the existing system: economic collapse (hyperinflation, default), military defeat (loss of territories, occupation), natural disaster (earthquake, flood, pandemic), technological catastrophe (nuclear accident, cyberattack on critical infrastructure).

Under conditions of collapse, existing institutions lose the ability to function. A group of persons possessing managerial competence and ready to accept responsibility forms a temporary coordination center. This center does not claim legitimacy through ideology or popular mandate, but acts on the basis of the fact of functioning: ability to restore basic services, ensure order, coordinate recovery.

Legitimacy is produced through result: the population and international community recognize the power of a structure that actually works. After stabilization of the situation, the temporary center initiates constitutional formalization of phronocratic principles through referendum or constituent assembly, using demonstrated effectiveness as grounds for trust.

The scenario requires a relatively short period for establishment and a longer one for institutionalization. Most unpredictable and depends on external circumstances. Requires preliminary preparation of personnel capable of taking on coordination under crisis conditions.

## Scope of Applicability

Phronocracy is most effective in spheres where quantitative or formalizable evaluation of results is possible, medium- and long-term time horizons are present, the price of errors is high and feedback is delayed, systemic coordination of multiple subjects is required.

Such spheres include macroeconomic policy, financial market regulation, energy, infrastructure development, scientific and technical planning, healthcare, education, evaluation of public programs.

Phronocracy is poorly applicable in spheres with blurred success criteria (culture, art, personal relationships) and short feedback cycles (operational management of enterprises, short-term trading). In these spheres, decisions must be made through market mechanisms, professional self-governance or individual choice.

## Distinction from Related Concepts

### Meritocracy

In meritocracy, power is distributed based on formal merits, education or recognized expertise. In phronocracy, competence is not presumed a priori based on diplomas or past achievements, but is confirmed exclusively through repeated empirical testing of forecasts under current conditions.

### Technocracy

Technocracy presumes priority of professional and technical knowledge without a mechanism for testing its applicability. Phronocracy treats any knowledge as a hypothesis subject to systematic testing, and does not protect expert status from falsification by practice.

### Epistocracy

Epistocracy proposes distributing political power proportionally to citizens' knowledge. Phronocracy separates knowledge from power: knowledge gives access to the executive circuit, but power is determined only by results of applying that knowledge.

### Futarchy

Futarchy proposes making decisions based on prediction markets. Phronocracy uses forecasts not for making decisions, but for evaluating managers who have already made decisions. Decisions are made by people bearing absolute responsibility, not by anonymous market participants.

## Historical Analogues

**The Confucian mandarin system in imperial China** represented meritocratic selection of officials through examinations regardless of origin. The system existed for more than a thousand years, providing China with periods of significant technological and cultural superiority. Deficiency: selection by knowledge of canonical texts, not by managerial results.

**Singapore under Lee Kuan Yew** demonstrated principles of pragmatic meritocracy: elite selection by ability, intolerance of incompetence, long-term planning. Result: transformation of a port city into one of the most developed economies in the world over several decades. Deficiency: dependence on the leader's personality, absence of institutionalized verification mechanisms.

**China after Deng Xiaoping's reforms** partially introduced the principle of performance into party hierarchy: official advancement depends on economic indicators of managed regions. Result: lifting hundreds of millions of people out of poverty, largest economic growth in history. Deficiency: absence of public competition of alternative models, political loyalty remains priority over result.

## Anticipation of Criticism

Phronocracy as a political model faces a number of typical objections. These objections proceed from premises that phronocracy itself does not share, which makes them irrelevant for evaluating the model from within its own logic.

### Objection 1: Definition of Success Metrics

Metrics are determined by the goal-setting body, formed outside the phronocratic hierarchy with mandatory significant representation of the supporting circuit. This is a constitutionally established function that cannot be usurped by the managerial circuit. The metrics themselves are subject to public discussion and can be changed through legitimate procedures.

Criticism of metrics is not criticism of phronocracy as such, since phronocracy is agnostic to the content of goals. Phronocracy answers the question "how to achieve set goals effectively", but not the question "which goals are correct".

### Objection 2: Metric Manipulation

Any evaluation system is subject to manipulation. Phronocracy neutralizes this through several mechanisms: publicity of forecasts (forecast is fixed before actions begin and cannot be changed post factum), competition of models (alternative subjects are interested in detecting competitors' manipulations), random audit (independent auditors receive material benefit from detecting falsifications).

Manipulations are possible, but their price (demotion or return to the supporting circuit when detected) makes them irrational in the long term. Additionally: the multi-level audit system and publicity of all data create multiple control points.

### Objection 3: Lowering Goals

Subjects will lower goals for the sake of guaranteed survival. This is a structural feature of any system with personal responsibility. Phronocracy solves this through competition for resources (a subject with lowered goals receives fewer resources with equal efficiency), experimental circuit (separate category of projects with high risk and high reward, where failure does not entail demotion if risks were correctly described in advance), public comparison (a subject systematically choosing safe strategies is visible against competitors and loses access to strategic resources).

Conservatism is the price of predictability. This is a conscious compromise, preferable compared to the cycle of populist promises and inevitable disappointments.

### Objection 4: Inequality of Starting Conditions

Subjects from wealthy regions have an advantage. Phronocracy does not guarantee equality of starting conditions. Normalization of results by starting conditions is possible, but any normalization formula is subjective and will become subject to manipulation.

Instead, phronocracy uses dynamic rotation: a subject does not manage one region for life. Periodically rotation occurs between regions of different complexity levels. A subject who has proven results in difficult conditions receives access to more resourced regions. This does not eliminate inequality of starting conditions, but prevents its consolidation.

### Objection 5: Toxic Audit Environment

The audit system creates tension and may cause witch hunts. The difference between phronocracy and corporate control systems lies in the price of deception. In corporations, the price of deception: dismissal, fine, in extreme cases prison. In phronocracy, the price of deception with catastrophic consequences is determined by the goal-setting body. This makes deception irrational at any reward.

Toxicity is ineliminable. But its level is lower than in a system where deception is unpunished or punished symbolically. Additional protection: multi-level audit, publicity of procedures, right to appeal to constitutional court.

### Objection 6: Algorithm Obsolescence

Any metric system becomes obsolete over time. Democratic constitutions are written rarely and become sacred texts. Politician metrics (gross domestic product, employment, inflation) have not changed for decades, although the world has changed radically.

The phronocratic verification algorithm is open to changes through the goal-setting body by qualified majority. Achieving consensus is difficult, but this is protection against impulsive changes. Metrics can be supplemented without changing basic rules. A new threat appeared - a new metric is added to the set. Old ones remain.

Dogmatization is inevitable. But the rate of obsolescence is lower than in systems with rigid ideological frameworks or dependence on electoral cycles.

### Objection 7: Non-Acceptance of Status by Supporting Circuit

The supporting circuit will not accept its status. No honesty of contract compensates for absence of political power. Latent discontent will accumulate.

Phronocracy does not promise eternal existence. The system is designed for an extended horizon - a period sufficient to solve critical tasks: climate stabilization or mitigation of consequences, technological breakthroughs in energy and production, demographic transition, creation of backup life support systems. After this, the system can be replaced. But by the time of replacement, critical problems will be solved, and civilization will receive a margin of safety for experiments with alternative forms of organization.

Additionally: the rights protection mechanism through significant representation in the goal-setting body and the right to appeal to the constitutional court reduce the likelihood of exploitation of the supporting circuit.

#### Constitutional Self-Liquidation Mechanism

Phronocracy does not claim eternity and does not create institutional obstacles to its own replacement. The system includes a built-in mechanism for legitimacy reassessment.

Periodically after establishment of phronocracy, a nationwide referendum on continuation of the system is automatically conducted. The referendum question is formulated neutrally: "Should the phronocratic governance system be continued?"

For continuation of the system, a threshold majority is required with a high turnout threshold. If the threshold is not reached, a process of constitutional reform is initiated with convening of a constituent assembly.

Additionally: any circuit (supporting, executive or strategic) can initiate an early referendum on termination of phronocracy in the presence of a petition signed by a significant share of members of that circuit. If petitions from several circuits reach the required threshold simultaneously, a referendum is conducted immediately.

The goal-setting body cannot block conducting a constitutional referendum, but can issue a voting recommendation with justification of its position.

This mechanism guarantees that phronocracy exists only as long as it maintains broad popular support. If the system has lost legitimacy - it is replaced constitutionally without the need for revolution or crisis.

### Objection 8: Dictatorship of Elite

Phronocracy is a dictatorship of function, not of elite. Elite presumes closedness, inheritance of status, corporate solidarity. Phronocracy is structurally hostile to this: status is not inherited and not transferred, access to the managerial circuit is open to anyone through result confirmation, subjects compete among themselves for resources, which excludes corporate solidarity, failure entails demotion regardless of past merits.

Criticism through the concept of "dictatorship" proceeds from a normative premise that any unequal power structure is immoral. Phronocracy does not share this premise. Power inequality is inevitable in any complex system. The question is not whether there is inequality, but by what principle it is distributed: by origin, wealth, popularity or result.

### Objection 9: Immorality of Physical Elimination

Immorality is a category relevant only within systems accepting morality as the foundation of legitimacy. Phronocracy is legitimized by effectiveness, not by morality.

Comparison of error prices: democratic politician failed a program - will not be reelected, will retire with privileges; autocratic official embezzled budget - will be transferred to another region; corporate leader collapsed a company - will receive compensation; phronocratic subject allowed catastrophic consequences - sanction is determined by the goal-setting body depending on jurisdiction.

In all systems except phronocracy, the price of incompetence at the highest level falls on the governed, not on the governors. Phronocracy inverts this relationship.

Additionally: physical elimination is not mandatory in all jurisdictions. In countries without the death penalty, life imprisonment is applied. This makes the model adaptable to different legal contexts.

### Objection 10: Ignoring Human Nature

Phronocracy does not attempt to change human nature. It creates a structure in which defects of human nature (corruption, incompetence, short-term thinking) are punished faster than they manage to inflict critical damage.

Ideal people do not exist. Ideal systems do not exist. The question is which system handles human imperfection least badly.

Democracy tries to distribute power so that no one can abuse it. Result: power is distributed so that no one can use it effectively. Autocracy concentrates power for efficiency. Result: power is concentrated in the hands of a subject whose competence is not verified. Phronocracy concentrates power for efficiency and simultaneously creates a mechanism of continuous competence verification.

### Objection 11: Formation of First Generation

If the system requires proven reputation for access to power, how is the first generation of managers formed without this reputation within phronocracy?

Answer: At the moment of phronocracy establishment, the first generation is formed through a transitional procedure different from the standard one.

**For the executive circuit:** Specialists with confirmed expertise in subject areas are selected (education, scientific publications, professional achievements, peer recognition). Managerial experience in preceding structures is considered but is not a sufficient criterion.

Key difference: each candidate formulates explicit public forecasts for the nearest period in their area of responsibility BEFORE taking office. These forecasts are fixed in an unchangeable format. After an established period, results are compared with forecasts. Those whose accuracy exceeded the threshold value remain in the executive circuit with reputation assignment. Those whose accuracy was lower return to the supporting circuit.

Thus, the first generation of the executive circuit undergoes a period of practical testing, rather than receiving the position for life based on formal merits.

**For the strategic circuit:** The first generation is formed from a subset of the executive circuit that demonstrated the highest results during the first period. Additionally, passing specialized tests for ability in long-term strategic planning and systemic thinking is required.

The first generation of the strategic circuit works under enhanced control: instead of the standard long-term horizon, a shortened horizon with more frequent intermediate verification is used. This compensates for absence of long-term reputation with increased verification frequency.

**Transitional period:** The first generation is transitional. Its task is not long-term governance, but creating a functioning verification system, accumulating primary data for reputational ratings and preparing a second generation that will already be selected by full phronocratic criteria.

The transitional period is estimated at several years duration. By the end of this period, a sufficient number of subjects will have accumulated reputational history within phronocracy, allowing transition to standard selection procedures.

### Objection 12: Short-Term Crises

Phronocracy with its long-term horizons may be ineffective under conditions of acute crises requiring immediate decisions (military threat, epidemic, financial collapse).

Phronocracy provides for an emergency governance regime: when a crisis is declared, the goal-setting body delegates expanded powers to the strategic circuit for a limited period. During this period, verification shifts to short-term metrics.

After the crisis ends, a retrospective evaluation is conducted: how well anti-crisis decisions corresponded to stated goals and whether they created long-term problems. Subjects whose decisions proved counterproductive in the long term are demoted or returned to lower circuits even with short-term success.

### Objection 13: Innovation Suppression

If every error is punished with demotion or return to a lower circuit, subjects will avoid any risks, which paralyzes innovation.

Phronocracy divides errors into two categories: errors of incompetence (forecast was impossible due to lack of knowledge or analysis) and errors of uncertainty (forecast was reasonable, but reality proved unpredictable).

Errors of incompetence are punished. Errors of uncertainty - are not, if the subject indicated risks and range of possible outcomes in advance.

Additionally: the experimental circuit allows testing radical innovations with high risk. Experiment failure does not entail demotion if the subject correctly described the probability of failure. Experiment success gives multiple reputation enhancement.

### Objection 14: Delayed Consequences and Horizon Problem

A subject can optimize indicators on the stated horizon, creating catastrophic consequences beyond this horizon. Environmental damage, demographic collapse, pension crisis, infrastructure degradation - all this manifests over a long time, when the responsible subject has already left the system.

Answer: Phronocracy uses a cascading system of forecasts with different temporal depths.

Each decision is accompanied not by one, but by several forecasts: short-term, medium-term, long-term. Evaluation is produced across all horizons simultaneously, but with different weights at different stages. At the moment of decision-making one weight, after several years the medium-term weight increases, after a longer period the long-term forecast acquires main weight.

Responsibility is retained beyond the term of office. A subject who left the managerial circuit remains responsible for long-term consequences of their decisions for life. If after a significant time after decision-making catastrophic consequences manifest that could have been foreseen, investigation is conducted retrospectively.

If the subject is alive - sanctions are applied in full. If the subject died - an official conclusion about the degree of their responsibility is published. This conclusion becomes part of the public archive of managerial decisions and is used for training future generations of managers: which approaches lead to long-term negative consequences, which signs indicate delayed risks.

The conclusion does not entail automatic responsibility for other subjects who used similar approaches. Each manager is responsible only for their own decisions. However, subjects continuing to apply approaches publicly recognized as leading to delayed catastrophes bear an increased level of control: their forecasts are verified with special attention to long-term risks, and at first signs of pattern repetition, preventive measures are applied.

Additionally: long-term forecasts are verified through a system of "tracking indicators" - intermediate metrics that must be within a certain corridor if the long-term forecast is correct. Deviation of tracking indicators triggers decision review before catastrophe occurs.

### Metacritique

Most objections to phronocracy proceed from one of three positions:

**Normative position:** the system is immoral because it does not correspond to certain ideas about justice. Phronocracy does not appeal to morality and does not defend itself through moral categories.

**Utopian position:** an ideal system must solve all problems without compromises. Phronocracy does not claim ideality and explicitly describes its limitations.

**Conservative position:** any radical change is more dangerous than existing problems. Phronocracy does not deny risks of changes, but considers risks of preserving the status quo higher.

Phronocracy can be effectively criticized only from within its own logic: through demonstration of internal contradictions of the model or through proposing an alternative system with a higher ratio of effectiveness to risks.

Criticism through external normative frameworks (morality, justice, human rights) is inapplicable to phronocracy, as these categories are not foundations of its legitimacy.

---

## Status of the Concept

Phronocracy represents a completed political model, applicable as an integral system of state organization. The model is not a universal solution to all managerial problems and does not claim moral superiority over alternative systems. Phronocracy is an engineering solution to the problem of managing complexity under conditions where existing systems demonstrate structural inability to function effectively.
